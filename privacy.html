Internet Privacy

What is Internet privacy and and how does it differ from Internet security?

What Is Internet Privacy?

Internet privacy involves the right for a person to control the spread of their personal data concerning the storing, repurposing, provisioning, and displaying that data for unintended purposes. Internet privacy is becoming a more pressing issue in today’s society because of the growing involvement of the internet into our daily lives. As it becomes harder to traverse through social norms without giving up sensitive information to the web, a growing number of people are concerned about what that could mean in the future.


What Is Internet Security?

Internet security consists of various security measures to protect your internet transactions. It prevents attacks targeted at browsers, network, operating systems, and other applications. Today, businesses and governments are prioritizing their security measure to protect themselves from malware originated from the internet. The U.S. economy between $57 billion and $109 billion due to cyber attacks and malware in 2016 and this number has been rapidly growing ever since. The main aim of Internet security is to set up precise rules and regulations that can deflect attacks that arise from the Internet.


Summary

Internet Privacy and security closely related terms that play an important role when we interact with the internet. Internet Privacy refers to the personal privacy that you’re entitled to when you display, store, or provide information regarding yourself on the Internet. Internet security consists of various security measures designed to keep transactions online secure. Internet security can exist without internet privacy, but it is not true the other way around. To have privacy these days you need to have security measure in place to protect your information. These terms differ in two main aspects. Let us take a bank account as an example. Security controls consist of controlling who can view your account information whereas privacy is controlling what and when people can access specific data.


Security controls are put in place to control who can access the information, while privacy is more granular, controlling what and when they can access specific data.
